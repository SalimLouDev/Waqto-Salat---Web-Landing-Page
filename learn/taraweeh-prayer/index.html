Taraweeh Prayer in Ramadan.

Taraweeh is the Ramadan night prayer Muslims pray after Isha, often in congregation, as a way to stand in worship, listen to Quran, and seek Allah's forgiveness.

Quick summary

  • When: after Isha during Ramadan, usually before Witr.
  • Purpose: standing in night prayer, reciting or listening to Quran, and seeking forgiveness.
  • Common practice: many communities pray it in congregation at the mosque.
  • Note: rak'ah counts differ, so follow your imam or madhhab.

What is Taraweeh prayer?

Taraweeh, also spelled Tarawih, is a special night prayer prayed in Ramadan after Isha. It belongs to the broader worship of Qiyam Ramadan, standing in prayer during the nights of Ramadan. Muslims gather for it to pray, hear Quran recitation, and revive the nights of the blessed month.

Taraweeh is not one of the five obligatory daily prayers. It is commonly taught as a Sunnah or voluntary Ramadan night prayer. Because detailed rulings can differ, it is wise to follow your imam, local scholars, or the madhhab you have learned.

When is Taraweeh prayed?

Taraweeh is prayed after Isha during Ramadan. In many mosques it is prayed before Witr, and the congregation may continue over the month until the Quran is completed or a local schedule is finished.

If you miss the mosque congregation, you can ask your local scholar about praying at home. The core meaning is to stand in night prayer during Ramadan with humility, remembrance, and hope for forgiveness.

Congregation and the Prophet's mercy

Hadith reports describe the Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, praying at night in Ramadan and people joining him. When more people gathered, he did not continue coming out every night, out of concern that the prayer might be made obligatory on the community.

Later, during the time of Umar ibn al-Khattab, Muslims were gathered behind one imam for Ramadan night prayer. This is why many communities today pray Taraweeh together in the mosque, while still understanding it as different from the five required daily prayers.

Rak'ah count and community differences

One of the most common questions about Taraweeh is the number of rak'ahs. You may see communities praying 8 rak'ahs, 20 rak'ahs, or another local arrangement, often followed by Witr. These differences are connected to scholarly interpretation and inherited community practice.

Do not turn the count into conflict

The night is for worship, Quran, and mercy. Follow your imam or madhhab and keep the masjid atmosphere gentle.

Quality matters

Pray with presence, listen carefully, and avoid rushing. Long recitation is common, but local communities vary.

Witr usually follows

Many communities pray Witr after Taraweeh. If you plan to pray more later, ask your imam how to handle Witr.

Common questions

Is Taraweeh obligatory?

No. Taraweeh is not like Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, or Isha. It is commonly taught as a Sunnah or voluntary Ramadan night prayer.

How many rak'ahs should I pray?

Follow your imam, local scholars, or madhhab. Many communities pray 8 or 20 rak'ahs before Witr, and the valid local practice should be respected.

Can women pray Taraweeh?

Yes, women may pray Taraweeh. Some pray at home and some attend the mosque where arrangements are available and appropriate.

What if I cannot stand for a long prayer?

Do what you are able to do. If standing is difficult, ask your local scholar about praying seated or shortening your personal prayer.
