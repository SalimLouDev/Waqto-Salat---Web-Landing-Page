Eclipse Prayer: solar and lunar salah.

Eclipse Prayer is the salah Muslims offer during a solar or lunar eclipse, turning a striking sign in creation into prayer, remembrance, supplication, and charity.

At a glance

  • When: during a solar or lunar eclipse until it clears.
  • Names: Kusuf is often used for solar eclipse, Khusuf for lunar eclipse.
  • Common form: two rak'ahs with extra standing and bowing.
  • Note: details differ by madhhab, so follow your imam or local scholars.

What is Eclipse Prayer?

Eclipse Prayer is a special prayer offered when Muslims witness a solar or lunar eclipse. Solar eclipse prayer is often called Salat al-Kusuf, and lunar eclipse prayer is often called Salat al-Khusuf. It is a moment to remember Allah, pray, ask forgiveness, and give charity.

The Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, corrected the idea that eclipses happen because of the death or birth of a person. He taught that the sun and moon are signs of Allah, and that Muslims should turn to prayer and supplication when they see an eclipse.

When is it prayed?

Eclipse Prayer is prayed while the eclipse is occurring. If the eclipse clears, the reason for the prayer has passed. In many communities, the mosque announces a congregational prayer if the eclipse is visible locally and the time is suitable.

There is no regular adhan or iqamah for Eclipse Prayer. Communities may call people together with a simple announcement such as a congregational prayer notice. If you are unsure what to do, follow your local imam or mosque announcement.

How Eclipse Prayer is commonly performed

In many Sunni communities, Eclipse Prayer is two rak'ahs, but it is longer than a normal two-rak'ah prayer. A common form has two standings and two bowings in each rak'ah, with recitation, ruku, standing again, recitation again, another ruku, then sujud. The second rak'ah follows the same pattern.

Long standing and recitation

The prayer is described in hadith as lengthy, with extended standing and recitation.

Extra bowing

The well-known form includes two bowings in each rak'ah, unlike the daily prayers.

Supplication and charity

The eclipse is also a time for du'a, seeking forgiveness, remembrance of Allah, and charity.

Solar and lunar eclipse differences

The spiritual lesson is the same for both solar and lunar eclipses: they are signs from Allah, not omens tied to human birth, death, or fortune. The name Kusuf is often used for solar eclipse and Khusuf for lunar eclipse, though usage can overlap in everyday speech.

Some legal details can differ between schools and communities, including whether the prayer is done in congregation, whether recitation is aloud or quiet, and how a sermon or reminder is handled afterward. Follow the imam where you pray.

Common questions

Is Eclipse Prayer only for solar eclipses?

No. Muslims also pray during lunar eclipses. The common terms are Kusuf for solar eclipse and Khusuf for lunar eclipse.

Can I pray it alone?

Many communities prefer praying it in congregation when possible, but if there is no congregation, ask your local scholar about the best way to pray it in your situation.

Is it connected to superstition?

No. The Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, rejected linking eclipses to the death or birth of people and directed Muslims toward worship instead.

What if I only catch part of the eclipse?

Pray while the eclipse is still present. If it has fully cleared, the specific occasion for Eclipse Prayer has ended.
