Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha Prayer.

Eid prayer is the special congregational prayer Muslims gather for on the two Eids: Eid al-Fitr after Ramadan and Eid al-Adha during the days of Hajj and sacrifice.

Quick summary

  • When: after sunrise and before Dhuhr, following your local Eid announcement.
  • Format: two rak'ahs with extra takbirs.
  • Khutbah: the sermon comes after the prayer.
  • Note: details differ by madhhab, so follow your imam or local scholars.

What is Eid prayer?

Eid prayer, also called Salat al-Eid, is prayed on the morning of the two annual Islamic festivals. Eid al-Fitr marks the completion of Ramadan and the beginning of Shawwal. Eid al-Adha falls in Dhul Hijjah and is connected to Hajj, the Day of Arafah, and the act of sacrifice for those who are able.

The prayer is usually performed in congregation, often in a mosque, large hall, or open prayer ground. It is a public act of worship, gratitude, and community. The exact legal ruling of attending Eid prayer is discussed differently across schools of law, so a Muslim should follow what they have learned from reliable teachers or their local community.

How Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha differ

Eid al-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr comes after Ramadan. Before the prayer, Muslims who are responsible for Zakat al-Fitr should make sure it has been given according to local guidance, so people in need can share in the day of Eid.

Eid al-Adha

Eid al-Adha comes in Dhul Hijjah. For those who are able and for whom it applies, it is connected to udhiyah or qurbani, the sacrifice performed after the Eid prayer during its appointed days.

When is Eid prayer prayed?

Eid prayer is prayed in the morning after the sun has risen and before Dhuhr. In practice, communities announce a local time so people can gather together. Since Eid dates depend on the Islamic lunar calendar and local moon-sighting or official announcements, always follow your mosque or recognized local Islamic authority for the exact day and time.

Many communities pray Eid al-Fitr slightly later to allow time for Zakat al-Fitr to be distributed. Eid al-Adha is often prayed earlier so people have time for the sacrifice afterward. These timing patterns are practical customs tied to the meaning of each Eid.

How is Eid prayer performed?

The Eid prayer is two rak'ahs. It begins like other prayers, but includes extra takbirs. Many Sunni communities follow the method of seven extra takbirs in the first rak'ah and five in the second, based on narrations recorded in the books of hadith. Hanafi communities commonly pray with three extra takbirs in the first rak'ah and three in the second. Both patterns are familiar across the Muslim world, so the simplest advice is to follow the imam in front of you.

The khutbah comes after the prayer, unlike Jumu'ah where the khutbah comes before the prayer. Classical reports describe the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, beginning with the Eid prayer and then addressing the people afterward. Eid prayer is also commonly performed without the regular adhan or iqamah, though organizers may still make announcements for crowd management.

Common questions

Do I need to pray Eid at home if I miss it?

Scholars differ on how missed Eid prayer should be handled. Some communities teach making it up in a simple form, while others emphasize that it is a congregational prayer tied to the appointed Eid gathering. Ask a trusted local scholar if you miss it.

Can women and children attend?

Many communities encourage families to attend Eid prayer because Eid is a communal act of worship and joy. Practical arrangements vary by mosque and venue, so check the local prayer announcement.

Is fasting allowed on Eid?

Muslims do not fast on the day of Eid al-Fitr or the day of Eid al-Adha. Eid is a day of gratitude, worship, food, family, and community.
