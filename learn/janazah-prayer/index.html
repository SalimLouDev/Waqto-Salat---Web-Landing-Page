Janazah Prayer: the Muslim funeral prayer.

Janazah prayer, also spelled Janaza, is the Muslim funeral prayer offered for a deceased Muslim, asking Allah for mercy, forgiveness, and an honored return to Him.

At a glance

  • Purpose: supplication for the deceased and a communal farewell.
  • Format: standing prayer with takbirs and no ruku or sujud.
  • Common form: four takbirs, followed by salam.
  • Note: details differ by madhhab, so follow your imam or local scholars.

What is Janazah prayer?

Janazah prayer is the funeral prayer Muslims offer for a deceased Muslim before burial. It is a prayer of du'a rather than a prayer with bowing and prostration. The congregation stands together, follows the imam through the takbirs, and asks Allah to forgive and have mercy on the deceased.

It is commonly taught as fard kifayah, a communal obligation. That means the Muslim community should make sure the prayer is offered. When some members fulfill it, the obligation is carried on behalf of the community.

Why attending matters

Attending Janazah prayer is both worship and support for the family and community. The Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, encouraged following the funeral and described a great reward for attending the prayer and remaining until burial. This is why Muslims often try to join Janazah even when they did not personally know the deceased.

The prayer also reminds the living of their own return to Allah. It gathers the community around mercy, humility, and sincere du'a instead of display or spectacle.

How Janazah prayer is performed

The basic form is a standing prayer behind the imam. There is no ruku and no sujud. The body is usually placed in front of the congregation, and the prayer is performed facing the qibla. If you are attending and do not know the details, stand with the congregation and follow the imam.

First takbir

Begin the prayer with the imam. Many communities recite Al-Fatihah here, while some communities follow a different introductory form.

Second takbir

Send salah and salam upon the Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him.

Third takbir

Make du'a for the deceased, asking Allah for forgiveness, mercy, pardon, and a generous reception.

Fourth takbir

Pause for brief supplication according to your community's practice, then end with salam following the imam.

Things to know before attending

Janazah prayer is usually simple and brief. Arrive with wudu if possible, dress respectfully, silence your phone, and follow the congregation. If the prayer is held after a regular salah, stay where the imam directs people to stand.

The details of hand placement, exact supplications, number of salams, and whether Al-Fatihah is recited can differ by school and local practice. The safest etiquette is to follow the imam and avoid correcting people in a moment of grief unless there is a clear need and proper knowledge.

Common questions

Does Janazah prayer have ruku or sujud?

No. It is performed standing, with takbirs and supplication, and without bowing or prostration.

How many takbirs are in Janazah prayer?

The widely known form has four takbirs. Classical reports mention the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, offering funeral prayer with four takbirs.

Can I attend if I do not know the du'a?

Yes. Follow the imam, make sincere du'a for the deceased, and learn the fuller supplications gradually.

What if the body is not present?

Scholars differ over absentee funeral prayer. Some allow it in specific cases, while others restrict it. Follow reliable local scholarly guidance.

Sources and further reading

  • Sahih al-Bukhari 1325 - reward for attending the funeral prayer and burial.
  • Sahih Muslim 945a - virtue of offering the funeral prayer and following the bier.
  • Sahih al-Bukhari 1333 - four takbirs in the funeral prayer for An-Najashi.
  • Sunan Abi Dawud 3196 - narration on four takbirs over the deceased.
