Tahajjud Prayer: voluntary night prayer.

Tahajjud is a voluntary night prayer prayed after Isha and before Fajr, often after waking from sleep, when the night is quiet and focused.

At a glance

  • When: after Isha and before Fajr.
  • Best known time: the later part of the night, especially after waking.
  • Common form: prayed in pairs of two rak'ahs, then Witr.
  • Category: voluntary night prayer, not one of the five obligations.

Editorial methodology

What is Tahajjud prayer?

Tahajjud is a voluntary prayer offered during the night after Isha and before Fajr. Many teachers use the word Tahajjud for night prayer after a person has slept and then wakes to pray, while Qiyam al-Layl can be used more broadly for standing in prayer at night.

It is not one of the five obligatory prayers, but it has a special place in Muslim worship because it asks for effort, sincerity, and quiet attention at a time when many people are asleep.

Tahajjud belongs to the wider family of Nafl prayers. It is commonly connected with Witr, while the five daily prayers always remain the priority.

When is Tahajjud prayed?

Tahajjud is prayed after Isha and before Fajr. The later part of the night is especially valued in many teachings, but the practical time depends on your sleep, work, family routine, and ability to wake before Fajr.

If waking in the last part of the night is hard, start with a small, realistic habit. You can also ask your local scholar how your madhhab distinguishes Tahajjud, Qiyam al-Layl, and night prayer before sleep.

How Tahajjud is commonly prayed

A common way to pray Tahajjud is in sets of two rak'ahs, then to end the night prayer with Witr. The number of rak'ahs can vary. Some people pray two rak'ahs and Witr; others pray longer when they have strength and time.

Begin small

Two calm rak'ahs with focus are a strong beginning, especially if you are building the habit.

Pray in pairs

Night prayer is commonly prayed two by two, then completed with Witr before Fajr.

Protect Fajr

Do not build a night routine that causes you to miss Fajr. Obligatory prayer comes first.

Tahajjud, Qiyam al-Layl, and Witr

Qiyam al-Layl means standing in prayer at night and can include night worship before or after sleep. Tahajjud is often used for night prayer after waking. Witr is the odd-numbered prayer that commonly closes the night prayer.

In Ramadan, Taraweeh is also a form of night prayer. Outside Ramadan, a person may keep a smaller private night prayer routine with Tahajjud and Witr. Follow your imam or trusted teacher for the details your community practices.

Common questions

Is Tahajjud obligatory?

No. Tahajjud is voluntary. The five daily prayers remain the priority.

Do I have to sleep first?

You can offer voluntary night prayer without sleeping first. Many teachers use Tahajjud more specifically for prayer after sleep and Qiyam al-Layl as the broader term. Ask your local scholar how your tradition defines them.

Should I pray Witr before or after Tahajjud?

If you expect to wake for Tahajjud, many people delay Witr until the end. If you fear missing it, pray Witr before sleeping.

What is a good beginner routine?

Wake a little before Fajr, pray two rak'ahs calmly, make du'a, then pray Witr if you have not prayed it already.
