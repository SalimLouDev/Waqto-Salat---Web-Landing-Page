Five Daily Prayers: Fajr to Isha.

The five daily prayers are the rhythm of Muslim life: Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha, each tied to its appointed part of the day.

Quick summary

  • Names: Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha.
  • Status: the required daily prayers for Muslims.
  • Obligatory rak'ahs: 2, 4, 4, 3, and 4 in order.
  • Timing: changes daily by sun position, location, and local method.

What are the five daily prayers?

The five daily prayers are Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha. They are the core required prayers of the Muslim day and are prayed at appointed times from before sunrise through the night.

Each prayer has a window of time connected to the movement of the sun. Because sunrise, sunset, and shadow lengths change by date and place, prayer times are not fixed clock times. They move through the year and should be followed according to reliable local calculation and community guidance.

The five prayers in order

Fajr

The dawn prayer, prayed before sunrise. Its obligatory prayer is 2 rak'ahs.

Dhuhr

The midday prayer, prayed after the sun passes its highest point. Its obligatory prayer is 4 rak'ahs.

Asr

The afternoon prayer, connected to the later part of the day and shadow length. Its obligatory prayer is 4 rak'ahs.

Maghrib

The sunset prayer, prayed after sunset. Its obligatory prayer is 3 rak'ahs.

Isha

The night prayer, prayed after twilight has passed. Its obligatory prayer is 4 rak'ahs.

Obligatory, Sunnah, and voluntary rak'ahs

The numbers above describe the obligatory rak'ahs of each daily prayer. Many Muslims also pray Sunnah or voluntary rak'ahs before or after some daily prayers, such as the Sunnah before Fajr or prayers connected to Dhuhr, Maghrib, and Isha.

These additional prayers are important in many communities, but they are not the same category as the required rak'ahs. If you are learning, start by understanding the five obligatory prayers clearly, then ask a teacher or local imam about the Sunnah prayers your community follows.

Why timing matters

Prayer is connected to time. The Quran points to prayer across the day and night, and hadith emphasizes offering the prayers at their stated times. This is why prayer time apps, mosque schedules, and local calendars matter in daily practice.

Waqto Salat helps with this practical part: prayer times, Qibla direction, widgets, and reminders that can be set before or after prayer time so they move as the daily schedule changes.

Common questions

Are the five daily prayers obligatory?

Yes. Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha are the required daily prayers for Muslims.

Do prayer times change every day?

Yes. Prayer times change with the sun, date, location, timezone, calculation method, and local guidance.

What is the difference between fard and Sunnah prayers?

Fard prayers are required. Sunnah prayers are additional prayers connected to the Prophet's practice and are taught with different levels of emphasis by scholars.

Which prayer comes first each day?

Fajr is the first daily prayer, followed by Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha.
