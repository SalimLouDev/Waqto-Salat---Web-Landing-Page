How each prayer time is determined

Prayer-time software begins with the date, latitude, longitude, elevation assumptions, and timezone. It then calculates the sun's position for that place. The five prayer windows are connected to observable stages of the day, while calculation methods translate some of those observations into numerical parameters.

Astronomical basis of the five daily prayer times

Asr settings and high-latitude rules

Asr juristic setting

The standard setting and the Hanafi setting use different shadow-length rules, so Asr can differ substantially even when every other setting matches.

High-latitude adjustment

When twilight does not reach the selected angle, apps may use the middle of the night, one-seventh of the night, or an angle-based portion of the night.

Timezone rules, daylight-saving changes, device location accuracy, and elevation assumptions can create further differences. This is why matching only the method name does not always produce the same timetable.

Which prayer time calculation method should you use?

  1. 1. Ask locallyFind the method or timetable followed by your mosque or recognized regional authority.
  2. 2. Match every settingConfirm the Asr rule, high-latitude rule, timezone, and location.
  3. 3. Compare the resultCheck several days against the trusted local timetable before adding offsets.

Calibration and local mosque times

Calibration applies a small per-prayer adjustment after the main calculation. It is useful when a trusted mosque timetable intentionally includes local margins or when a community follows an authority whose published result differs slightly from the selected app method. Calibration should refine a correctly configured method, not hide a wrong timezone, location, or Asr setting.

Frequently asked questions

Why do prayer times differ between apps?

Apps may use different calculation methods, juristic settings, location data, timezone handling, or manual offsets.

Can Waqto Salat match local mosque times?

Waqto Salat includes calibration controls so you can align prayer times more closely with local community guidance.

Which calculation method should I use?

Use the method followed by your local mosque or recognized authority, then confirm the Asr and high-latitude settings.

Why are Fajr and Isha difficult at high latitudes?

Normal twilight angles may not occur during parts of the year, so an approved high-latitude adjustment is needed.
