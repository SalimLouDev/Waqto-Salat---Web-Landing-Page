Purpose and scope

The Learn library gives clear introductions to Salah: what a prayer is, when it is offered, its shared method, and common beginner questions. We write for readers who need an understandable starting point and direct links to the references behind important claims.

The guides do not issue fatwas or replace a teacher. When a practice depends on madhhab, congregation, circumstance, or local guidance, the page says so instead of presenting one view as the only valid one.

Source hierarchy

Primary texts

Relevant Quran passages and hadith reports are linked directly whenever they support the guide's central claims.

Scholarly context

Widely recognized differences are described carefully, with readers directed to their madhhab or local scholar for application.

Technical references

Calculation pages use established astronomy and software documentation, while noting that local timetables can apply adjustments.

Differences of opinion

Salah details can differ across recognized schools and communities. Examples include Asr shadow settings, Witr classification and Qunut, some prayer time boundaries, and the wording used for types of night prayer. We identify meaningful differences in the section where they matter.

We avoid turning a short educational page into a ruling for every reader. For a personal question, follow a qualified scholar who understands your school, location, and circumstances.

Review and update process

  1. 1. Define the reader question. Each guide is organized around the exact timing, purpose, method, and terminology readers search for.
  2. 2. Check the references. Source links are verified and claims are kept within what those references support.
  3. 3. Preserve valid nuance. Madhhab differences and local practice are stated where one universal answer would be misleading.
  4. 4. Record meaningful updates. A page's reviewed and structured-data modification dates change when its substance is reviewed or improved.

Corrections and feedback

Clear corrections improve the library. To report a source problem, unclear wording, broken link, or an important scholarly distinction, email contact@waqtosalat.com and include the page URL and the passage you are referring to.
